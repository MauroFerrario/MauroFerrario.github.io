Title: Colloidal supra-nanostructures for emerging technologies¶

Abstract¶

A new class of materials has been recently synthesized by self-assembly of semiconductor nanocrystals in coherent structures with a long range order. The appealing feature of these materials is that they can be synthesized in a variety of different geometric arrangements in space. This thesis proposes to investigate the electronic structure of these materials using state-of-the-art computational tools.

Title: Band Engineering in Nanowires¶

Abstract¶

Semiconductor Nanowires are of great interest for their possible application in photonic, sensing, and energy harvesting devices. Due to the efficient stress relaxation they allow to combine more compounds into their structure. This thesis aims at studying the evolution of the electronic structure of nanowires with nanowire design modifications: nanowire dimension, nanowire superlattices, core-shell nanowires, and quantum-dots in nanowires. Notes —– Possible collaborations with research groups in Rome, Pise and Brazil.

Title: Multiscale simulations of oxide-based nanomaterials for applications to catalysis and clean energy production¶

Abstract¶

For many important applications in the production of clean energy it is important to have access to systems able to break and transform molecules (for example to absorb CO2, or to break methanol to produce hydrogen) and transfer easily atoms and electrons from one part of the system to another one. A class of reducible metal-oxides seem to have the necessary properties for achieving these goals. The thesis tackles the problem of how the physical and chemical processes occurring at the surfaces of metal-oxide nanostructures can be properly described. The interaction between a metal-oxide nanoparticle surface and simple molecules will be studied through ab-initio methods and multiscale techniques. The energy barriers of the different physical processes will be calculated to provide an understanding of the catalytic efficiency of the surface as a function of its structure and composition. The work will be conducted in strict collaboration with experimental groups growing, characterizing, and studying these materials.

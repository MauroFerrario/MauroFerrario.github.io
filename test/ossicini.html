Title: Carrier Multiplication Processes in Low Dimensional Systems¶

Abstract¶

The carrier multiplication (CM) is a non-radiative recombination mechanism that leads to the generation of a large number of electron-hole pairs after absorption of a single photon. If favored by the quantum confinement of the electron density, CM can be as fast as or even faster than phonon emission, extending the portion of the solar spectrum converted into energy. The expected outcome of this process is therefore an improvement in photovoltaic performance induced by integration of nanopatterned structures inside the solar cell device. In this thesis we will quantify CM dynamics considering different low dimensional nanosystems. Conditions that maximize efficiency of CM processes will be quantified. Effects induced by nanocrystals interplay on CM dynamics will be analyzed1,2 . [1] M. Govoni, I. Marri and S. Ossicini, Nature Photonics 6, 672–679 (2012) [2] I. Marri, M. Govoni and S. Ossicini, J. Am. Chem. Soc. 13257, 136 (2014)

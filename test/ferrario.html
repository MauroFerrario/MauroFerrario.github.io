Title: High resolution Model to Biogeochemical forecasting service in Mediterranean Sea¶

Abstract¶

Copernicus <http://www.copernicus.eu> is the European system for monitoring the Earth for supporting policymakers and public authorities who need the information to develop environmental legislation and policies (e.g. for environmental protection, climate change mitigation, etc). The Copernicus monitoring system consists of a large suite of observing systems (satellites, autonomous observing systems) and model predictions and past simulations. Within the Copernicus Program the National Institute of Oceanography and Experimental Geophysics (OGS - <http://www.inogs.it> ) manages the Biogeochemical Forecasting Service of the Mediterranean Sea ( www.medeaf.inogs.it ) and contributes to the development of the next generation of biogeochemical forecast models. One of recent scientific improvements of the OGS model consists of the increase the resolution up to 1/24 of degree. This improvement comes with a substantial increase of the computational cost, therefore new hardware and software techniques have to be developed to optimize the forecast modeling system and to take back the time to solution within the required time. Among the possible solutions, the introduction of a new smart data distribution among the processor of HPC Cluster to divide Mediterranean Basin is promissing considiring the availability of the new CINECA <http://www.hpc.cineca.it> supercomputing cluster (MARCONI) that uses the newest Intel 72 core Processor KNL. The design of new data distribution will take into consideration the specificity of the hardware of the new cluster (especially the new KNL network and memory management), the time performance requested and the complexity and dishomogeneity of the Mediterranean Sea shape. Further, the new solution will tackle the optimization of the numerical scheme that solves the advection-diffusion equations of the tracers.

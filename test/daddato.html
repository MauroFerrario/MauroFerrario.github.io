Title: “Beating the superparamagnetic limit”: magnetic stabilization of core-shell nano particles realized by physical synthesis¶

Abstract¶

The subject of this thesis is the study of core-shell nanoparticles (NPs) realized with a NP source operating at the SESAMO laboratory. The NPs will be grown with controlled core size and by varying the shell thickness, in order to obtain stable magnetic stable NPs even at RT. This behavior is of strategic interest in applications, like for instance in developing high density magnetic memories. The NP assembly (Fe@CoO and Co@NiO NP) will be thoroughly investigated with XPS, AFM, SEM and STEM. The magnetic characterization will be performed with SQUID (in collaboration with external research groups) and possibly with XMCD at synchrotron laboratories.

Title: Tree-level amplitudes for a relativistic scalar particle in curved space¶

Abstract¶

Path integrals, as integrals over trajectories, were first proposed by Wiener to study the Brownian motion. Feynman reintroduced the tool as an alternative to operatorial methods in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. However the field of application of Feynman particle path integrals is much wider, as realized by Feynman himself. In particular they may be employed as a technique—-alternative to conventional second-quantized methods—-to compute scattering amplitudes, and effective actions, in relativistic Quantum Field Theories. This technique is dubbed “the Worldline Formalism.”

The present proposal deals with the study and the application of worldline path integrals to compute the perturbative expansion of the dressed propagator of a scalar particle in curved space. In particular we will consider the associated set of scattering amplitudes, that concern processes involving two scalar particles, and an arbitrary number of gravitons.

Note¶

The student(s) will acquire some familiarity with functional methods in quantum field theory, with general covariant formalism, and with particle path integrals tecniques.

Title: Scalar/spinor contributions to the gluon effective action in the worldline formalism¶

Abstract¶

Path integrals, as integrals over trajectories, were first proposed by Wiener to study the Brownian motion. Feynman reintroduced the tool as an alternative to operatorial methods in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. However the field of application of Feynman particle path integrals is much wider, as realized by Feynman himself. In particular they may be employed as a technique—-alternative to conventional second-quantized methods—-to compute scattering amplitudes, and effective actions, in relativistic Quantum Field Theories. This technique is dubbed “the Worldline Formalism.”

The present proposal concerns the study and application of new worldline path integral tecniques to compute the inverse mass expansion of the one-loop gluon effective action generated by a massive scalar/spinor particle coupled to a non-abelian gauge field. We expect to obtain results that may improve on the efficiency of perturbative calculations.

Note¶

The student(s) will acquire some familiarity with functional methods in quantum field theory, with non-abelian gauge theories, and with particle path integrals tecniques.

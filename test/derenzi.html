Title: Growth and characterization of self-assembled graphene nanoribbon¶

Abstract¶

Graphene nanoribbons (GNRs), a quasi one-dimensional narrow strip of graphene, are raising great interest as they exhibit width-tunable band-gaps, while maintaining the outstanding properties of graphene. Covalent self-assembly of suitable molecular precursors is a powerful approach to obtain atomical control on GNRs width, shape and chemical composition, opening the way to a precise tuning of their properties. The activity foresees the study of different types of GNR, grown both on Au thin films and single crystals. In particular, the Au(788) vicinal surface will be exploited to induce a macroscopic alignment of the GNR orientation. The morphology, the electronic and vibrational properties of these systems will be investigated, by means of XPS, ARUPS, HREELS, LEED, STM and, possibly, by synchrotron radiation based techniques.

Note¶

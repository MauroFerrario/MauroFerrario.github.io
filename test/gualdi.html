Title: The simulated atmospheric circulation over the Euro-Mediterranean region: sensitivity to the Sea-Surface Temperature forcing¶

Abstract¶

Objectives: investigate the characteristics of the atmospheric circulation over the Mediterranean basin and explore its link with the global ocean sea-surface temperature (SST).

Work to be done: the main parameters of the atmospheric circulation, such as, for instance, temperature, sea-level-pressure, wind velocity, precipitation, etc, will be used along with the most advance statistical techniques of the analysis of the variance. The analysis will be performed mainly using the outputs of a set of model simulations performed with two different atmospheric general circulation models (GCMs) forced with the same interannual varying SSTs taken from observations. In particular, the importance of the SST and its variability at interannual time scales in determining the features of the atmospheric anomalies over the Mediterranean basin (including surrounding areas) will be analysed and assessed.

Expected results: the analysis will allow an understanding of the main features of the atmospheric circulation over the Mediterranean region and will improve the comprehension of the mechanisms governing the anomalies associated with the variations in global sea-surface temperature at interannual timescales.

Title: The simulated atmospheric circulation over the Euro-Mediterranean region: sensitivity to the model horizontal resolution¶

Abstract¶

Objectives: explore the sensitivity of the main features of the simulated atmospheric circulation over the Euro-Mediterranean region to the horizontal resolution of a general circulation model (GCM).

Work to be done: analyse and compare the atmospheric circulation as simulated with an atmospheric general circulation model (GCM) implemented with two different horizontal resolutions (1deg and 1/4 deg) and forced with interannualy varying SST taken from observations, focusing on the Euro-Mediterranean region. The analysis will be performed applying the most advance statistical techniques of analysis of the variance.

Expected results: the analysis will be especially aimed at identifying the role of model resolution in reproducing the main features of the observed climate (both mean conditions and variability) and in representing the main dynamical processes that govern the variability from intraseasonal to interannual time scales.

Contact: Guido Goldoni, Silvio Gualdi¶

Title: Aspects of the mean climatology and variability of temperature, salinity and currents in the Indian Ocean in a high resolution global ocean re-analysis¶

Abstract¶

Objective: to evaluate the mean climatology and variability of the CMCC C-GLORS products (global ocean reanalysis at high resolution) over the Indian Ocean.

Work to be done: the work will consist in the analysis of available re-analysis outputs at high horizontal resolution, focusing on characterising the main features of the mean climatology and variability of the Indian Ocean. This is a very important and crucial area for the tropical climate and its variability, due to the link with the Asian monsoon and its relationship with the Pacific Ocean.

Expected results: the analysis will provide a characterization of the main features of the oceanic circulation in the Indian Ocean, its variability and its link with the atmospheric variability.

Contact: Guido Goldoni, Silvio Gualdi¶

Title: Basic effects of Tropical variability on the Euro-Mediterranean region¶

Abstract¶

Objective: evaluate the linear component of the atmospheric response to large anomalies in the Tropics and its possible effects on the Euro-Mediterranean climate.

Work to be done: this study will examine the response to tropical heat forcings in a linear primitive equation model of the atmosphere. The linear response will be computed considering different basic states, in order to explore the characteristics of the Tropics-mid latitudes teleconnections in correspondence of different climatic mean states.

Expected results: the analysis will improve our understanding of the basic mechanisms underpinning the teleconnection between Tropics and midlatitudes and their influence on the climate variability in the Euro-Mediterranean region.

Title:  Reactions at materials interfaces activated by mechanical stresses: fundamental understanding and applications in tribochemistry¶

Abstract¶

During the course of a chemical reaction, reactants need to overcome an energy barrier to transform into products. The energy for this process is typically provided by heat or light. A fundamentally different way of initiating or accelerating a reaction is the use of mechanical forces. Understanding reaction kinetics in the presence mechanical forces represents a fascinating fundamental problem with important applications in different fields, such as “tribochemistry”, where chemical reactions involving lubricant or environmental molecules are activated at the interface between two solids in relative motion. The present PhD thesis will focus on ab initio molecular dynamics simulations of (tribo)chemical reactions involving solid lubricants, such as graphene, molybdenum disulphide and carbon-based films, interacting with water molecules. The lubricating properties of these materials are, in fact, highly affected by humidity for reasons not fully understood.

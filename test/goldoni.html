Title: Eletronic properties of radial heterostructures¶

Abstract¶

Radial heterostructures are a new class of nanomaterials, where the electronic systems is confined to a wrapped around two-dimensions sheet, also called a quantum tube. Due to their peculiar topology, phase coherent electronics, photovoltaics, thermoelectricity are some of the most promising applications. This thesis will focus on the electronic and optical properties of these revolutionary nano-materials. The activity is material oriented, it offers the possibility to get familiar with state-of-art modelling of semiconductor heterostructures, and has a theoretical-computational character. The activity will make use of a software developed in-house. Specific goals of the thesis may include the prediction of electronic properties in complex configurations, spin-orbit effects, modelling of thermoelectric behaviour.

References:

    1. Buscemi et al., Tailoring the core electron density in modulation-doped Core-Multi-Shell Nanowires, Nanotechnology 27, 195201 (2016)

Title: Scattering dynamics of indirect excitons¶

Abstract¶

Scattering and tunneling of a quantum particle is a text-book topic, but scattering of a composite particle formed by two or more interacting quantum particles –a quantum composite- is little investigated, yet, a far-reaching problem, with implications in fields as diverse a semiconductor physics, molecular scattering, and nuclear fission. When scattering against an external field (an impurity, an electrostatic field, a magnetic barrier, etc) all sorts of internal dynamics of the pair may be excited. This thesis will investigate scattering processes of a particular class of quantum composite, namely, indirect excitons, long-lived optical excitations forming in certain semiconductor heterostructures. The thesis, with a theoretical/computational character, and may involve the study of the dynamic of single IX, pairs of IXs, or an IX gas forming a Bose-Einstein condensate. Students will became familiar with state-of-the-art techniques in time-dependent quantum simulations as well as semiconductor material description.

References:

    1. Grasselli et al. Time-dependent scattering of a composite particle: A local self-energy approach for internal excitations, Phys. Rev. B 94, 125418 (2016)
    1. Grasselli, Methods for the time-dependent dynamics of indirect excitons, Master thesis, A.Y. 2012/2013

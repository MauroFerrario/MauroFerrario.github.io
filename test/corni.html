Title: Simulating the interaction between biomolecules and inorganic surfaces/nanoparticles¶

Abstract¶

Recent combinatorial biotechnologies have shown that the molecular recognition capability of proteins can be specifically oriented toward inorganic surfaces and nanoparticles. Such specificity could lead to the elaboration of new technologies in several fields, from nanoelectronics to biomaterials. Moreover, the interaction between proteins and nanoparticles is pivotal in determining the biological response to nanoparticles. DNA interacting with surfaces is also the subject of intense researches, e.g., as a possible inexpensive way to determine epigenetic DNA mutations. However, the principles regulating protein-surface and DNA surface interactions are poorly understood. The aim of our work is to theoretically address protein-surface and DNA surface interactions, applying and extending the tools of computational physics/chemistry/biology. In particular, a multiscale approach involving ab initio calculations, Molecular Dynamics, Coarse-grained and Docking-like techniques is being developed and applied to relevant problems in the field.

Title: Molecular and nanoplasmonics by an atomistic perspective¶

Abstract¶

Plasmons at the nanoscale are useful in several applications such as photovoltaics and spectroscopy. There are several basic open issues that are still to be understood in this field. On one hand, it is still unclear how to identify a nanoconfined plasmon excitation by an atomistic quantum perspective. On the other hand, when a molecule is close to a plasmonic nanosystem new phenomena can emerge, such as a modified photophysics for the molecule, that calls for new theoretical models. The aim of the work is to address these open issues from a first-principles perspective, developing and applying the required theoretical approaches
